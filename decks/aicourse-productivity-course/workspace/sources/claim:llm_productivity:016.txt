Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:summarisation:001
Statement: Specifying audience, format, and extraction targets in a summarisation prompt produces substantially better results than a generic 'summarise this' instruction. Structured prompting techniques can improve performance across tasks by between 6% and more than 30%.

Source: "Smarter AI Through Prompt Engineering: Insights and Case Studies from Data Science Application"
URL: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.00337
Type: paper (arXiv preprint, 2026)

Excerpt:
"Structured prompting techniques can improve performance across various tasks by between 6% and more than 30%, according to case studies in healthcare, materials science, financial services, and business intelligence."

Additional supporting context from the Prompt Engineering Guide (promptingguide.ai):
"For summarization specifically, instructions should outline what the AI should focus on and how it should structure the summary, and the desired length should be specified."

A vague prompt like "Summarize this incident" may yield technical detail or irrelevant background, but something more specific like "Summarize this cyber incident for board review in 2 bullets: (1) Business impact, (2) Next steps. Avoid technical jargon" is significantly more effective.
