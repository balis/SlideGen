Full excerpt (composite from multiple reports):
"Samsung Electronics Co. is banning employee use of popular generative AI tools like ChatGPT after discovering staff uploaded sensitive code to the platform, dealing a setback to the spread of such technology in the workplace."

"Within less than 20 days of allowing ChatGPT access, Samsung experienced three separate incidents where engineers exposed confidential company information."

The three incidents:
1. An engineer entered Samsung source code into ChatGPT when looking for a solution to a bug.
2. Another recorded a company meeting, transcribed it, and inputted the transcription into ChatGPT to create meeting notes.
3. A third used ChatGPT to optimize a test sequence for identifying yield and defective chips.

"Samsung announced plans to develop their own internal AI system with proper data controls and security measures."

Note: Samsung later re-enabled employee access to ChatGPT with guardrails. This incident is widely cited as a canonical example of accidental corporate data exposure via consumer AI tools.
