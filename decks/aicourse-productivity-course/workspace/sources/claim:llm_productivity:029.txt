Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:habits:004
Statement: LLMs default to a generic, middle-of-the-road writing voice because they are trained to satisfy the broadest audience; preserving a personal voice requires explicit countermeasures such as providing style examples, using the AI as an editor rather than a drafter, or iterative refinement.

Source: Scale AI Blog
Title: Using LLMs While Preserving Your Voice
URL: https://scale.com/blog/using-llms-while-preserving-your-voice
Retrieved: 2026-05-27

Full excerpt:
"Large language models are essentially complicated autocomplete systems tuned to please the maximum number of users, and as such, they default by design to middle-of-the-road word choices and unimaginative syntax."

"LLM-generated text often differs from human writing in grammatical (overused structures), lexical (overused words), and stylistic (predictable patterns) ways."

"Rather than using AI as a writer, use it as an analyzer — have it flag places where clarity could be improved, and ask the AI to explain why — then fix the issue your own way."

Additional strategies cited:
- "Craft detailed instructions specifying desired voice, tone, audience, and structure, and incorporate brand-specific language guidelines into prompts."
- "In creative writing, 2-5 samples suffice to elicit strong style imitation through in-context learning" (few-shot approach).
- "Use iterative prompting techniques to refine output toward your unique style."
