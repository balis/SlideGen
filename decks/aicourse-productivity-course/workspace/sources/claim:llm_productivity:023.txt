Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:planning:003
Statement: LLMs are unreliable at date and calendar arithmetic, with per-primitive
accuracy ranging from near-zero to perfect depending on model and prompting conditions.

Source: "PRIMETIME: Limits of LLMs in Temporal Primitives"
Authors: (arXiv preprint)
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.16155
HTML: https://arxiv.org/html/2504.16155v2

Abstract excerpt:
"This paper introduces PRIMETIME, a synthetic generator that supports both
benchmarking and fine-tuning of two primitive operations underlying temporal
reasoning in Large Language Models (LLMs): parsing and arithmetic on datetimes."

"the primitives themselves prove individually unreliable, with per-primitive
accuracy ranging from near-zero to perfect across models and prompting conditions."

Additional context from the paper:
- Datetime operations such as adding days are non-trivial due to the variable
  number of days per month and leap years.
- While datetime arithmetic is trivial in any programming language, the LLM's
  ability to perform it internally matters for composed tasks like event planning.
- Only state-of-the-art proprietary frontier models perform reliably across all
  tasks; smaller and open models show severe inconsistency.
- Small fine-tuned models can reach frontier-level accuracy, suggesting the
  weakness is learnable, not fundamental — but out-of-the-box LLMs remain
  unreliable for date arithmetic.
