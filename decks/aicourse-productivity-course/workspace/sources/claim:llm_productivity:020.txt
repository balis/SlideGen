Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:summarisation:005
Statement: Goal-oriented prompt formulation — specifying intent, domain, and demand — significantly improves LLM performance compared to open-ended prompts, and applies directly to summarisation tasks where specifying the decision context focuses the output on actionable information.

Source: "Towards Goal-oriented Prompt Engineering for Large Language Models: A Survey"
Published: January 2024 (arXiv preprint)
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.14043

Excerpt:
"A goal-oriented prompt formulation, which guides LLMs to follow established human logical thinking, significantly improves the performance of LLMs. The approach can be summarized into three key aspects: intent, domain, and demand."

Intent defines the task's goal, demand specifies the detailed requirements, and domain identifies the information source relevant to the task. Applied to summarisation, this maps directly to the "extract X for audience Y in format Z" pattern: X = intent (what decision or action the summary serves), Y = domain context (who will read it and what they know), Z = demand (output structure and constraints).
