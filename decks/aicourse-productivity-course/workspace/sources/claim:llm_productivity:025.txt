Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:planning:005
Statement: On TimeBench, even GPT-4 shows a significant performance gap versus humans
on temporal reasoning, with event temporal reasoning showing a 25.2% gap.

Source: "TimeBench: A Comprehensive Evaluation of Temporal Reasoning Abilities
in Large Language Models"
Authors: Zheng Chu, Jingchang Chen, Qianglong Chen, Weijiang Yu, Haotian Wang,
Ming Liu, Bing Qin
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.17667
Published at: ACL 2024 (https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.66.pdf)

Key excerpts:
"The experimental results indicate a significant performance gap between the
state-of-the-art LLMs and humans, highlighting that there is still a considerable
distance to cover in temporal reasoning."

"LLMs exhibit capability discrepancies across different reasoning categories."

Specific results by category:
- Symbolic Temporal Reasoning: GPT-4 achieves exceptional performance,
  particularly in Date Arithmetic (100%), suggesting strong capabilities in
  abstract date calculation.
- Commonsense Temporal Reasoning: GPT-4 performs well with an 84.1% average,
  only 8.0% behind humans.
- Event Temporal Reasoning: presents the most significant challenge, with a
  substantial 25.2% gap between LLMs and humans.

Note: The 100% score on Date Arithmetic in TimeBench for GPT-4 appears to
contrast with PRIMETIME findings. This likely reflects different task complexity:
TimeBench's date arithmetic may test simpler symbolic operations, while PRIMETIME
tests more diverse and complex datetime primitives across varied formats and
prompting conditions. The key takeaway for the course is that date reasoning
reliability varies dramatically by task complexity and model.
