Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:summarisation:003
Statement: In multi-document summarisation, LLMs show a significant primacy effect: summaries are more semantically aligned with whichever article appears first in the input, meaning document order shapes the output's framing and emphasis.

Source: "Input Order Shapes LLM Semantic Alignment in Multi-Document Summarization"
Authors: (arXiv preprint)
Published: December 2025
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02665

Excerpt:
"A significant primacy effect for BERTScore across all stances, showing that summaries are more semantically aligned with the first-seen article, with Position 1 differing significantly from Positions 2 and 3."

"Position bias describes the tendency of models to disproportionately prioritize information appearing at specific positions within the input sequence, leading to the neglect of important details elsewhere."

The study used abortion-related news triplets with different input orderings to prompt Gemini 2.5 Flash to generate neutral overviews, evaluating each summary against source articles using ROUGE-L (lexical overlap), BERTScore (semantic similarity), and SummaC (factual consistency). The LLM showed a consistent preference for the first input article, shaping both the framing and semantic similarity patterns.
