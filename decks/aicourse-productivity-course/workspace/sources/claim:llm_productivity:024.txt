Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:planning:004
Statement: Date tokenization is a hidden bottleneck for temporal reasoning in LLMs,
with BPE fragmentation causing accuracy drops of up to 10 percentage points.

Source: "Date Fragments: A Hidden Bottleneck of Tokenization for Temporal Reasoning"
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.16088
PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.16088

Key excerpts:
"Modern BPE tokenizers fragment calendar dates into meaningless components.
For example, '20250312' becomes tokens '202', '503', '12', splitting what should
be a coherent temporal unit into disconnected tokens, inflating token counts and
obscuring date structure."

"Excessive fragmentation correlates with accuracy drops of up to 10 points on
uncommon dates like historical and futuristic dates."

Additional findings:
- LLMs employ an emergent mechanism to reassemble scattered date components,
  stitching together fragments of month, day, and year components for temporal
  reasoning, with larger models accomplishing this reconstruction faster than
  smaller ones.
- LLMs follow a different assembly sequence than humans: year -> month -> day,
  diverging from typical human interpretation patterns.
- The authors introduced DateAugBench, a benchmark with 6,500 examples covering
  context-based date resolution, format-invariance puzzles, and arithmetic
  operations across historical, contemporary, and future timeframes.
