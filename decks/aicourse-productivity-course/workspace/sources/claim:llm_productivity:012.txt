Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:document:002
Statement: Combining positive 'do' instructions with negative exclusion constraints (e.g., 'fix grammar and clarity; do not change numbers or technical terms') significantly reduces redundancy and unwanted modifications while maintaining output consistency.

Source: From Prompts to Templates: A Systematic Prompt Template Analysis for Real-world LLM Apps
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.02052
Retrieved: 2026-05-27

Full excerpt:
"Developers frequently use exclusions as the constraints to refine outputs such as excluding irrelevant content, reducing hallucinations, and narrowing search space for generation."

"Combining 'Do' instructions with 'Don't' instructions, like exclusion constraints, significantly reduces redundancy while maintaining high output consistency."

Additional finding from the same paper:
"Developers commonly put the profile/role and their directive at the beginning of the prompt, establishing the model's identity and task intent, while examples are typically at the end."

The paper analysed real-world LLM application prompt templates and identified seven common components: Profile/Role, Directive, Context/Workflows, Output Format/Style, and Constraints.
