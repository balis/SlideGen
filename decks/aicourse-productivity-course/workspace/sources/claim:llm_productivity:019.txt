Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:summarisation:004
Statement: Shuffling document order across multiple LLM runs and comparing outputs is a practical mitigation for position bias; cyclic and full-permutation methods that average predictions across orderings reduce the effect of positional bias on summary content.

Source: "Input Order Shapes LLM Semantic Alignment in Multi-Document Summarization"
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02665
Published: December 2025

Additional source: Position bias mitigation literature surveyed in the above paper and in:
"Revisiting Zero-Shot Abstractive Summarization in the Era of Large Language Models from the Perspective of Position Bias"
URL: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2401.01989

Excerpt:
"To counter position biases, cyclic and full-permutation methods have been proposed that average predictions across permutations. Prompting techniques can effectively direct model attention to specific positions as a way to reduce positional bias."

"To overcome position bias where different ordering of inputs leads to different results, you can prompt the LLM multiple times and shuffle the inputs between the runs, then average the results."

The practical "shuffle check" for non-technical users: run the same multi-document summary prompt twice with documents in different order. If the summaries differ materially, the model is relying on position rather than content — revise the prompt or treat divergent points with caution.
