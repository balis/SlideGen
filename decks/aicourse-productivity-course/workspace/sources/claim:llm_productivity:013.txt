Claim ID: claim:llm_productivity:document:003
Statement: Register refers to linguistic variation associated with the situational use of language, and prompting LLMs with register-analysis-based style descriptors achieves a better balance between style-transfer strength and meaning preservation than open-ended style descriptions.

Source: Steering Large Language Models with Register Analysis for Arbitrary Style Transfer
Authors: (arxiv preprint, May 2025)
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.00679
Retrieved: 2026-05-27

Full excerpt:
"Register refers to linguistic variation associated with the situational use of language" characterised by "situational context, linguistic features, and functional relationships."

"prompting LLMs to analyze exemplars' style using Biber's multidimensional register analysis (MDA) framework" provides "a structured and effective way to generate accurate target style descriptors."

"Biber's register analysis framework is widely available online and used in educational and linguistic contexts, LLMs are likely to have been exposed to examples of such analyses during training."

"Our prompting variants show similar to improved style transfer strength compared to the baselines; Our prompting variants show a large gain in meaning preservation across the tasks."

"RG is on the Pareto frontier across all data splits, achieving a good balance between style transfer strength and meaning preservation."
