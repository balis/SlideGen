Excerpt (LLM02:2025 - Sensitive Information Disclosure):
"LLMs, particularly when embedded in applications, pose the risk of exposing sensitive
data, proprietary algorithms, or confidential details through their outputs. This can
result in unauthorized data access, privacy violations, and intellectual property
breaches."

"Negligent inclusion of sensitive data in training sets results in disclosure through
the model's outputs."

Sensitive Information Disclosure rose from #6 (2023 edition) to #2 (2025 edition) due
to growing attack techniques and real-world incidents. LLMs can memorize and reproduce
fragments of training data through both verbatim memorization (directly regurgitating
strings) and semantic memorization.

Supporting context on consumer vs enterprise:
OpenAI's enterprise privacy page (https://openai.com/enterprise-privacy/) states that
ChatGPT Enterprise, Business, Edu, and API data is not used for training by default.
Consumer-plan (Free, Plus) inputs may be used to train models unless users explicitly
opt out via the privacy portal.
