Source: "Detecting and Correcting Reference Hallucinations in Commercial LLMs and Deep
Research Agents." arXiv:2604.03173, April 2026.

URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03173

Excerpt:
"GhostCite benchmarks 13 LLMs across 40 domains (375K citations) with hallucination
rates from 14% to 95%. ChatGPT-3.5 hallucinates 39.6% to 55% of citations in
literature reviews, while GPT-4 still produces 18%-28.6% fabricated citations. Citation
hallucination persists even when models have access to web search, with 3-13% of URLs
fabricated in retrieval-augmented settings."

Supporting source: "Compound Deception in Elite Peer Review: A Failure Mode Taxonomy
of 100 Fabricated Citations at NeurIPS 2025." arXiv:2602.05930, February 2026.
This study found 100 AI-generated hallucinated citations in 53 NeurIPS 2025 papers
(~1% of accepted papers), classifying them as: Total Fabrication (66%), Partial
Attribute Corruption (27%), Identifier Hijacking (4%), Placeholder Hallucination (2%),
Semantic Hallucination (1%).

Supporting source: JMIR Mental Health study on citation fabrication rates in mental
health research using GPT-4o. URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC12658395/
